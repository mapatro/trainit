Start here — your ClearEaty growth job, explained

Welcome! This course teaches you to do real digital marketing for ClearEaty using AI tools — step by step. Read this page once, then start Chapter 1 and work in order.

Your mission

Get more of the right people to install ClearEaty and keep using it.

ClearEaty is a free app that scans a food barcode or ingredients label and gives an honest 1–10 health score, with every ingredient explained. Your goal is more installs, more people taking their first scan, and more people who keep scanning and tell a friend. You’ll do this across search, content, app-store listings, social, Reddit, video, ads, and creators — and you’ll use AI to produce every asset.

The 4 non-negotiable rules

These protect the brand. Break them and we can get the app removed or mislead people. Follow them in every post, page, and ad.

1 · Informational, never medical. ClearEaty tells people what’s in their food. Never write “cures,” “treats,” “detox,” “toxic,” or “guaranteed weight loss.” No health claims, ever.
2 · It’s free — don’t invent a price. The app is free, no ads, no sign-up, no subscription. Never advertise a paid plan or “premium.” Your win is installs and active users, not revenue.
3 · US store, diaspora engine. The app is available in the US. Our fastest early growth comes from the Indian-diaspora network — WhatsApp, community groups, regional-language content, and diaspora creators. Use both.
4 · Be honest and helpful. Show real scans and real scores. On Reddit and in communities, lead with genuine help and follow each group’s rules — never spam. Always disclose paid creator partnerships (#ad).

How the course works

There are 14 chapters. Do them in order — each builds the growth engine on the last. Every chapter has the same rhythm:

  1. A short idea — what the skill is and why it matters (2–4 sentences).
  2. ● Do this — concrete steps that produce a real ClearEaty asset.
  3. AI prompt boxes — copy-paste prompts you run in your AI tool to create the asset (see below).
  4. ✓ Check yourself — a pass/fail list so you know the asset is actually done and good.
  5. ↗ More — a link if you want to go deeper.
By the end of each chapter you will have produced a finished asset — a positioning statement, a landing page, an ad set, a content calendar, and so on. Save every asset (next section).

How to use the AI prompt boxes

The dark boxes are prompts you paste into an AI assistant (Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini). They look like this:

› You are a marketing strategist. ClearEaty is a free app that gives packaged foods an honest 1–10 health score… Create 3 buyer personas with [the details the chapter asks for]. Keep all claims informational, not medical.
  1. Click Copy on the box.
  2. Paste it into your AI tool (Claude / ChatGPT / Gemini).
  3. Fill in the placeholders — anything in [brackets] is for you to replace with real ClearEaty details or your previous answers.
  4. Read the result, refine it (“make it shorter,” “more for Indian parents,” “remove any medical wording”), and save the final version as your asset.

Always sanity-check AI output. AI can invent facts, stats, or pricing. Verify anything factual, and make sure nothing slipped into a medical claim before you publish.

What you need (tools)

An AI assistant

Claude, ChatGPT, or Gemini — your co-pilot for every asset.

A place to save work

Google Drive / Docs + Sheets for assets, calendars, and trackers.

A simple design tool

Canva (free) or similar for graphics, screenshots, and Reels covers.

Account access

Ask the founder for: the brand kit, app-store links, and any ad/analytics logins when a chapter needs them.

Tools and prices change — if a chapter names a specific tool, confirm it’s current before relying on it, and never lock our work into one paid tool.

Your weekly rhythm

  1. Work through chapters in order (roughly 1–3 per week at the start).
  2. Ship the asset from each chapter and save it in the shared Drive folder.
  3. Once the app is live, review the numbers weekly (Chapter 13) and pick the one biggest thing to improve next.
  4. Send a short weekly update to the founder: what you shipped, what you learned, what’s next.

Save your work like this

Create one shared folder, ClearEaty Marketing, with sub-folders matching the chapters (01-positioning, 02-funnel, …). Drop each finished asset in its folder. That folder becomes ClearEaty’s marketing playbook — and proof of your work.
