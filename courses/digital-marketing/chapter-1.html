1

Know the product — the truth sheet

You can’t market what you can’t describe in one breath. ClearEaty lets anyone scan a barcode or photograph an ingredients label and get an honest 1–10 health score with every ingredient explained in plain language — plus healthier picks. It is free, no ads, no sign-up, no tracking. Store availability today is the US; the early growth engine is health-curious US users plus the founder’s Indian-diaspora network.

Reality check vs. older briefs: ClearEaty is free with no paid subscription right now. Don’t advertise a price or “premium tier.” Your job is installs and active users, not subscription revenue (monetization comes later). Remember: ClearEaty is an information tool, not medical advice. Never write “cures,” “treats,” “detox,” or “guaranteed weight loss.” Keep it about knowing what’s in your food.

● Do this
  1. Write a 5-line “product truth sheet”: what it does, who it’s for, what it costs (free), what it is NOT (not medical), and the real taglines.
  2. Save the real taglines to reuse everywhere: Know what’s in your food, Snap before you eat, Every ingredient explained, No ads. No tracking.
2

The one-line promise

A great promise is specific, visual, and instantly understood. Use AI to generate options, then pick one you can defend.

ai prompt · value-prop variants
› You are a brand strategist. ClearEaty is a FREE mobile app (US, iOS+Android, no ads, no sign-up) that gives packaged foods an honest 1–10 health score from a barcode scan or a photo of the ingredients label, explaining every ingredient in plain language and suggesting healthier picks. Audience: health-curious US shoppers and the South Asian diaspora (parents, label-readers). Write 10 one-line value propositions: visual, under 12 words, no medical claims (no cure/treat/detox). Then pick the best 3 and explain why each would make someone install.
✓ Check yourself
  • You have one primary promise written down
  • It is under ~12 words and makes no medical claim
  • A stranger could repeat it after hearing it once
3

Who it’s really for — personas

Market to specific humans, not “everyone.” Build 2–3 personas you’ll picture every time you write.

ai prompt · build personas
› Create 3 detailed buyer personas for ClearEaty (free food-label health-score app; US store; strong South-Asian-diaspora network). For each: name, age, country/city, family situation, health goal, frustration with current food choices, which apps/sites/communities they already use, where they spend time online (incl. Reddit subs, Instagram, WhatsApp groups), and the ONE message that would make them install today. Keep everything informational, not medical.
✓ Check yourself
  • 3 personas, each with a real online “watering hole” you can reach
  • At least one diaspora-specific persona
  • Each persona has a single trigger message
4

Competitor wedge

Know who you’re compared to (Yuka, Bobby Approved, HealthifyMe, generic calorie counters) and where ClearEaty wins: honest plain-language scoring, free with no ads/tracking, and a fast “scan → score → swap” loop.

ai prompt · competitor teardown
› Compare ClearEaty against Yuka, Bobby Approved (Bobby’s Healthy Shop), and HealthifyMe for a US + South-Asian-diaspora audience. Table: positioning, price model, data sources, strengths, weaknesses, and the gap ClearEaty can own. Then give me 3 sharp “unlike X, ClearEaty…” statements I can use in marketing (truthful, no medical claims).

More: April Dunford on positioning

5

Lock the positioning statement

● Do this
  1. Fill this template: For [persona] who [need], ClearEaty is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [alternative], ClearEaty [differentiator].
  2. Pin it at the top of a shared doc — every future asset must agree with it.
ai prompt · finalize positioning
› Here are my 3 personas [paste] and my chosen promise [paste]. Write one tight positioning statement using: “For [persona] who [need], ClearEaty is a [category] that [benefit]. Unlike [alternative], ClearEaty [differentiator].” Then list 5 message pillars I should repeat across all channels.
✓ Check yourself
  • One positioning statement everyone agrees on
  • 5 message pillars saved for reuse
